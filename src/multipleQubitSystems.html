Introduction

Encoding information in quantum states is efficient when examining systems of more than one qubit.

Unlike classical systems, the state space of a quantum system grows exponentially with the number of particles. Thus, when we encode computational information in quantum states of a system of n particles, there are vastly more possible computation states available than when classical states are used to encode the infor- mation.
These large state spaces corresponding to small amounts of physical space can be used to speed up computation.
A surprising and unintuitive aspect of quantum systems is that often the state of a system cannot be described in terms of the states of its component pieces: these are the entangled states.

Most states in a multiple-qubit system are entangled states; they are what fills the vast quantum state spaces.

In classical physics, the possible states of a system of n objects, whose individual states can be described by a vector in a two-dimensional vector space, can be described by vectors in a vector space of 2n dimensions. $$ n=5 \\ a,b,c,d,e \in (0,1) \\ \text{dim tot}= 2 \times 5 $$ Classical state spaces combine through the direct sum.
The combined state space of n quantum systems is much larger. The vector spaces associated with the quantum systems combine through the tensor product , resulting in a vector space of: $$ dimensions= 2^n $$ Pag 32 Direct Sums of Vector Spaces

Direct Sums of Vector Spaces

Direct sum: given two vector spaces V and W with bases A,B: $$ A= \{ | \alpha_1 \rangle ,| \alpha_2 \rangle ,...,| \alpha_N \rangle \} \\ B= \{ | \beta_1 \rangle ,| \beta_2 \rangle ,...,| \beta_M \rangle \} \\ T = V \oplus W \text{ Direct sum} \\ $$ The base of T is: $$ C= A \cup B \\ C= \{ | \alpha_1 \rangle ,| \alpha_2 \rangle ,...,| \alpha_N \rangle, | \beta_1 \rangle ,| \beta_2 \rangle ,...,| \beta_M \rangle \} \\ dim(C)=dim(A)+dim(B) \\ \text {For same: } | v \rangle \in V , | w \rangle \in W \\ | c \rangle \in C \\ | c \rangle = | v \rangle \oplus | w \rangle $$ Addition and scalar multiplication are defined by performing the operation on the two component vector spaces separately and adding the results. In general: $$ \begin{pmatrix} x_1\\ p_1\\ \end{pmatrix} \oplus \begin{pmatrix} x_2\\ p_2\\ \end{pmatrix} \oplus \begin{pmatrix} x_3\\ p_3\\ \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} x_1\\ p_1\\ x_2\\ p_2\\ x_3\\ p_3\\ \end{pmatrix} $$ Single component has dimension 2 thus total dimension is 2 x N, in this case dimension is 6.

Tensor Products of Vector Spaces

Given two vector spaces V and W with bases A,B: $$ A= \{ | \alpha_1 \rangle ,| \alpha_2 \rangle ,...,| \alpha_N \rangle \} \\ B= \{ | \beta_1 \rangle ,| \beta_2 \rangle ,...,| \beta_M \rangle \} \\ T = V \otimes W \text{ tensor product} \\ $$ Tensor product is an abstract binary operator that satisfies the following relations: $$ (| v_1 \rangle + | v_2 \rangle) \otimes | w \rangle= | v_1 \rangle \otimes | w \rangle + | v_2 \rangle \otimes | w \rangle \\ | w \rangle \otimes (| v_1 \rangle + | v_2 \rangle) = | w \rangle \otimes | v_1 \rangle + | w \rangle \otimes | v_1 \rangle \\ (a| v \rangle) \otimes | w \rangle =| v \rangle \otimes (a| w \rangle)= a(|v \rangle \otimes | w \rangle) $$ All elements of T can be written as: $$ L = N \times M \\ | t \rangle= l_1 (| \alpha_1 \rangle \otimes | \beta_1 \rangle) + \\ l_2 (| \alpha_2 \rangle \otimes | \beta_1 \rangle) + ...+ \\ l_N (| \alpha_N \rangle \otimes | \beta_1 \rangle) + ...+ \\ l_{N+1} (| \alpha_1 \rangle \otimes | \beta_2 \rangle) + ...+ \\ l_{L} (| \alpha_N \rangle \otimes | \beta_M \rangle) \\ $$ Thus we have \( L = N \times M \) components for ket \( | t \rangle \) beeing T nm-dimensional space.

Tensor Product with Qbit

V and W are vector spaces corresponding to a qubit $$ V \text{ has th base } \{ | 0 \rangle ,| 1 \rangle \} \\ W \text{ has base } \{ | 0 \rangle ,| 1 \rangle \} \\ $$ The tensor product of two single-qubit is $$ T = V \otimes W \text{ has base: } \\ \{ | 0 \rangle \otimes | 0 \rangle, | 1 \rangle \otimes | 0 \rangle, | 0 \rangle \otimes | 1 \rangle, | 1 \rangle \otimes | 1 \rangle \} \\ | v \rangle = a_v | 0 \rangle + b_v | 1 \rangle \\ | w \rangle = a_w | 0 \rangle + b_w | 1 \rangle \\ | v \rangle \otimes | w \rangle = \\ a_v a_w| 0 \rangle \otimes | 0 \rangle + b_v a_w| 1 \rangle \otimes | 0 \rangle + \\ a_v b_w| 0 \rangle \otimes | 1 \rangle + b_v b_w| 1 \rangle \otimes | 1 \rangle $$

Tensor Product and inner product

In V and W is defined an inner product, in \( T = V \otimes W \).
In T we define: $$ | v1 \rangle , |v2 \rangle \in V \text{ and } | w1 \rangle , |w2 \rangle \in W \\ (\langle v2 | \otimes \langle w2 |) \cdot ( |v1 \rangle \otimes | w1 \rangle) = \langle v2 | v1 \rangle \langle w2 | w1 \rangle $$

Tensor Product and basis

The tensor product of two unit vector is a unit vector.
Given orthonormal basis: $$ A= \{ | \alpha_1 \rangle ,| \alpha_2 \rangle ,...,| \alpha_N \rangle \} \\ B= \{ | \beta_1 \rangle ,| \beta_2 \rangle ,...,| \beta_M \rangle \} \\ T = V \otimes W $$ The basis fo T is also orthonormal: $$ \{ | \alpha_i \rangle | \beta_j \rangle \} \\ dimension = dim(V) \times dim(W) $$ The tensor product of n two-dimesional vector spaces $$ T = V_1 \otimes V_2 \otimes ...\otimes V_n \\ dim(V_i)=2 \\ dim(T) = 2^N $$

Entangled States

Given $$ A= \{ | \alpha_1 \rangle ,| \alpha_2 \rangle ,...,| \alpha_N \rangle \} \\ B= \{ | \beta_1 \rangle ,| \beta_2 \rangle ,...,| \beta_M \rangle \} \\ T = V \otimes W $$ \( | t \rangle \in V \otimes W \) can always be expressed as linear combination of their basis but most part of \( | t \rangle \) cannot be written as the tensor product of elements of V and W.
These states are called entangled states.

The State Space of an n-Qubit System

let V i be the vector space with base \( \{ | 0 \rangle ,| 1 \rangle \} \)
The tensor product of N vector space v is: $$ T = V_1 \otimes V_2 \otimes ...\otimes V_n \\ Base= \\ \{| 0 \rangle_n ,| 0 \rangle_{n-1} ,...,| 0 \rangle_0,| 0 \rangle_0, \\ | 0 \rangle_n ,| 0 \rangle_{n-1} ,...,| 0 \rangle_0,| 1 \rangle_0, \\ | 0 \rangle_n ,| 0 \rangle_{n-1} ,...,| 1 \rangle_0,| 0 \rangle_0,\\ | 0 \rangle_n ,| 0 \rangle_{n-1} ,...,| 1 \rangle_0,| 1 \rangle_0, \\ ... \\ | 1 \rangle_n ,| 1 \rangle_{n-1} ,...,| 1 \rangle_0,| 1 \rangle_0 \} \\ $$ Dropping the subscript $$ Base= \\ \{| 0 \rangle ,| 0 \rangle ,...,| 0 \rangle,| 0 \rangle, \\ | 0 \rangle ,| 0 \rangle ,...,| 0 \rangle,| 1 \rangle, \\ | 0 \rangle ,| 0 \rangle ,...,| 1 \rangle,| 0 \rangle, \\ | 0 \rangle ,| 0 \rangle ,...,| 1 \rangle,| 1 \rangle, \\ ... \\ | 1 \rangle ,| 1 \rangle,...,| 1 \rangle,| 1 \rangle \} \\ $$ We have \( 2^{n} \) vectors.
Since the tensor product space corresponding to an n-qubit system occurs so frequently throughout quantum information processing, an even more compact and readable notation is $$ Base= \\ \{| 0...00 \rangle , | 0...01 \rangle , | 0...10 \rangle , | 0...11 \rangle , ..., | 1...11 \rangle \} \\ $$ or $$ Base= \\ \{| 0 \rangle , | 1 \rangle , | 2 \rangle , | 3 \rangle , ..., | 2^n-1 \rangle \} \\ $$

Matrix Notation

For example a two qubit state: $$ | s \rangle = \frac{1}{2} | 00 \rangle + \frac{i}{2} | 01 \rangle + \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}| 11 \rangle $$ became: $$ \begin{pmatrix} \frac{1}{2}\\ \frac{i}{2}\\ 0 \\ \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\\ \end{pmatrix} $$

Bell Babis

Entangled States

Let's take two single qubit state \( | s \rangle,| t \rangle \).
If we do the tensor product we have: $$ | s \rangle= a_1| 0 \rangle + b_1| 1 \rangle \\ | t \rangle= a_2| 0 \rangle + b_2| 1 \rangle \\ | r \rangle= | s \rangle \otimes | t \rangle = \\ (a_1| 0 \rangle + b_1| 1 \rangle) \otimes ( a_2| 0 \rangle + b_2| 1 \rangle) = \\ a_1a_2| 00 \rangle + a_1b_2| 01 \rangle + a_2b_1| 10 \rangle + b_1b_2| 11 \rangle $$ This in general, but if we take a particular state, for example: $$ | r_0 \rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} (| 00 \rangle + | 11 \rangle) \\ $$ The solutions riquire: $$ a_1b_2=0 $$ but this implies that \( a_1=0 \) or \( b_2=0 \) impling also \( a_1a_1 =0 \) or \( b_2 b_2=0 \)

This means that we could not find two single state to generate the state \( | r_0 \rangle \)

States that cannot be written as tensor product of single qubit states are called entangled states
